If there is no area for that year, there was no data.

The color scheme I used was rainbow from highest to lowest government debt to percentage of GDP, with red being the highest and violet being the lowest. I felt that a rainbow was the best way to color the data because of the high number of countries in the data set. Trying to separate the countries by geographic region would've just resulted in a lot of European countries with the same color and then a few countries with different colors.

While the beeswarm and area graphs are similar, I feel that an area graph is much easier to intepretm, as the flow of values is linear instead of having a new circle for each year. Also, breaking out the colors assists in visualizing and associating values of government debt to percentage of GDP with their corresponding colors long the rainbow spectrum.
